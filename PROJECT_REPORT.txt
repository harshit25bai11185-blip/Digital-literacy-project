TASK 4:
18) A common problem caused by poor digital communication is the "vague delegation" issue. In a hypothetical scenario, a student project team is finalizing a presentation via group chat. One member quickly types, "I'll handle the final slides." The sender means they will write the concluding slides. However, the rest of the team interprets this to mean the sender is formatting the entire final deck. Consequently, no one edits the main body, resulting in a disjointed, error-filled presentation and a poor grade.

This issue stems from the lack of precision common in quick digital messages. To prevent this, the communication needed specific parameters. The sender should have stated, "I will draft the conclusion on slides 15-18." Furthermore, implementing a quick summary message outlining everyone's explicit responsibilities before logging off would have caught the misunderstanding early and ensured everyone was aligned.
